Call for Papers

Kernel extensions are seeing a renewed interest in the research community with the advent of eBPF (extended Berkeley Packet Filter). eBPF has been gaining popularity in industry for its safety and efficiency in programming end-host network and OS stacks.

The workshop will bring together a diverse and interdisciplinary audience to learn, network, and exchange ideas. We welcome contributions including, but not limited to:

  • Better approaches to eBPF program safety verification.
  • Emerging use cases in networking, security, and CPU scheduling.
  • Techniques to optimize performance of eBPF programs.
  • Security implications of kernel extensions.
  • Case studies and real-world deployments.

Submission Instructions

We are looking for two types of submissions:

  • Research papers: Up to 6 pages. Submissions must be original, unpublished work.
  • Poster abstracts: Up to 2 pages. Designed for early-stage findings and works in progress.

Please submit your papers via ebpf26.hotcrp.com.
