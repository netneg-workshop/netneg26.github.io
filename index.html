eBPF'26 Workshop

Welcome to the 2026 Workshop on eBPF. This event brings together researchers and practitioners to discuss the latest advancements in kernel extensibility, safety, and performance.

The workshop will be held in conjunction with SOSP 2026.

Call for Papers

Kernel extensions are seeing a renewed interest in the research community with the advent of eBPF (extended Berkeley Packet Filter). eBPF has been gaining popularity in industry for its safety and efficiency in programming end-host network and OS stacks.

We welcome contributions including, but not limited to:

  • Better approaches to eBPF program safety verification.
  • Emerging use cases in networking, security, and CPU scheduling.
  • Techniques to optimize performance of eBPF programs.
  • Security implications of kernel extensions.

Submission Instructions

We are looking for research papers (up to 6 pages) and poster abstracts (up to 2 pages). Please submit your papers via ebpf26.hotcrp.com.
